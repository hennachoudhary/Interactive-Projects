Intimate Partner Homicide Declines in Queens

Queens is New York City’s only borough to witness a decline in intimate partner homicide from 2015 to 2016, according to New York City Domestic Violence Fatality Review Committee’s 2017 annual report.




Overall, the total number of intimate partner homicides within the city increased by twelve from 2015 to 2016, with the greatest number of victims falling within the age range of 25-29. The most commonly used weapon was a knife, with a firearm being the second most frequently used method of murder. The most common victim was an adult female, with there being nine more adult female victims in 2016 than 2015, although adult female victims was at a higher rate than adult male victims throughout 2010 to 2016.

The Bronx, which had the highest rate of homicide out of all five boroughs during both years, had six more intimate partner murders in 2016 than 2015. In Manhattan, the rate stayed stationary with three recorded homicides in both 2015 and 2016. Brooklyn and Staten Island’s both saw their murder rates increase by five between the years.
